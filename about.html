About the Designer

I’m Trevlin Olckers — a corporate graphic designer working at the intersection of communication, strategy, and visual storytelling.

My work lives in real environments, not just inspiration boards. I design for internal communications, marketing teams, and operational spaces where clarity matters and deadlines are real. Over the past few years, I’ve worked within the telecoms and corporate environment, supporting multiple brands and business units with design that helps people understand, align, and act.

I don’t see design as decoration. I see it as translation.

Translation of strategy into visuals.
Translation of complex information into something usable.
Translation of ideas into something people actually engage with.

Most of my work focuses on internal campaigns, corporate communications, digital content, and print materials — the kind of design that needs to be clear, structured, and purposeful. Posters, presentations, intranet visuals, social content, and campaign assets are where I’ve built my foundation. This space has taught me how to design within constraints, collaborate with stakeholders, and deliver work that supports business goals, not just aesthetics.

At the same time, I’m intentionally expanding.

I’m growing into UI and web design — learning how digital environments shape behaviour, navigation, and clarity. I’m building confidence in layout systems, front-end basics, and designing experiences that work as well as they look.

Right now, I’m focused on becoming a designer whose work is trusted in corporate environments and adaptable in digital ones.

Not just someone who makes things look good.
Someone who makes communication work.

I’m building a body of work that reflects growth — from graphic design into digital, from execution into thinking, from tasks into ownership. Every project is part of that trajectory.
